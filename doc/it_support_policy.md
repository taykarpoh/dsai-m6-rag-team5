# IT Support Policy

## How to Get Help

- **Self-service first.** The IT knowledge base covers the most
  common requests: password reset, MFA reset, VPN setup, printer
  setup, conferencing-tool basics. Most issues can be resolved
  faster from the knowledge base than from a ticket.
- **File a ticket** through the IT Support portal for anything not
  covered by self-service. Tickets are routed to the right team
  automatically based on the category.
- **Walk-up support** is available at the office IT desk between
  09:00 and 17:00 on working days. Bring your laptop and a clear
  description of the problem.

## Response Targets

- **P1 (cannot work at all)** — first response within 30 minutes,
  resolution target 4 working hours.
- **P2 (significantly degraded)** — first response within 2 working
  hours, resolution target 1 working day.
- **P3 (minor or new request)** — first response within 1 working
  day, resolution target 5 working days.
- **P4 (informational)** — best effort.

If you cannot work at all and you have not had a P1 response within
30 minutes, page the IT on-call manager via the incident tool.

## Acceptable Use

Company devices are for company work and reasonable personal use.
Reasonable personal use does not include: file-sharing of copyrighted
material, mining cryptocurrency, hosting personal services, or
installing software that is not on the approved-software list. The
approved-software list lives in the IT knowledge base; request
additions through the portal.

## Software Requests

Most common software is pre-approved and can be installed through the
self-service catalogue. Anything not in the catalogue requires a
ticket; the IT team will check licensing and security before
approval. Approval target is 5 working days.

## Hardware Requests

- **Replacement** for failed hardware: file a P2 ticket. A loan
  device is provided within one working day for laptops.
- **Upgrade** outside the standard refresh cycle: requires manager
  approval and a business case in the ticket.
- **Accessories** (keyboards, mice, headsets, monitors for the
  office): pre-approved up to a per-employee budget. Remote workers
  see the Expenses guide for the home-office allowance.

## Security Reporting

If you suspect a security incident — phishing, lost device,
suspicious access — report it through the Security portal AND the
IT team within 1 hour. Do not wait to confirm; over-reporting is
preferred to under-reporting.
